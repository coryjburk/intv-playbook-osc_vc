## What this tool is

The Ops/Supply Chain Manager Interview Playbook is a **single HTML file** that runs entirely in your browser — no account, no internet connection, and no installation needed. Double-click the file (or open the live link) and it works. Everything is self-contained, and your data stays in your browser.

It was built for candidates preparing for manager-level roles, with content spanning the full range from foundational concepts to expert-level judgment calls, written at the standard of a real manager interview.

| | |
|---|---|
| 📚 **Study** | Browse the modules — questions, scenarios, frameworks, drills, and more — organized by category and difficulty. |
| 🎙️ **Practice** | Record spoken answers with a live timer and filler-word counter, or type them in. Every question, scenario, and leadership prompt has a Practice button. |
| 🤖 **Get AI coaching** | Generate a structured prompt to paste into any AI assistant (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) and receive expert-level feedback — with on-screen steps that walk you through it. |
| 📊 **Track readiness** | Paste the AI's scored feedback back in and watch your competency dashboard build over time — one logged rep at a time. |

**Content at a glance:** 120 questions · 30 scenarios · 20 frameworks · 18 quant drills · 20 leadership prompts · 15 battlecards · 20 red flags.

## Quick start

**Up and running in 60 seconds:**

1. Open the **live link** above, or **double-click** the `index.html` file — it opens in your default browser. No server needed.
2. First-time visitors see a **"New here? How this works in 4 steps"** banner at the top. Read it, then dismiss it with the **×** — it won't show again.
3. Watch the **hero stats count up** in the header: 120 Questions, 30 Scenarios, 20 Frameworks, 18 Quant Drills, 20 Leadership Prompts. These confirm the tool loaded.
4. Click any **tab** across the top to navigate to that module.
5. Click any **card** to expand it and read the full content.
6. When you're ready to practice, click the **Practice button** on any card.

> 💡 **Best browser:** Use **Chrome or Edge**. The microphone (speech-to-text) feature requires Chrome or Edge and works only on `https://` or `localhost`. On the live GitHub Pages link the mic works normally. If you run the file locally (a `file://` path), the mic may be blocked — just type your answer instead; the coaching workflow is identical.

## Reading the cards

## Practice mode

Every question, scenario, and leadership prompt has a **Practice** button that opens a full-screen modal for a complete rep.

Inside the modal:

| Element | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Timer** | Starts at 00:00 when you begin recording. Helps you stay within a realistic 2–4 minute answer. |
| **Mic / Recording** | Click the mic to start speech-to-text; click again to stop. Requires Chrome or Edge. Degrades gracefully to a text box if the mic is unavailable. |
| **Filler-word counter** | Updates live as you speak or type. |
| **Transcript box** | Your spoken words appear here automatically. You can also type directly. Text is editable — clean it up before generating the coaching prompt. |
| **Save notes** | Saves the current transcript to your browser, attached to that specific question, and reloads it next time you open it. |

**Tracked filler words:** `um` · `uh` · `like` · `you know` · `kind of` · `sort of` · `basically` · `actually` · `literally` · `I mean` · `right`

**Recording your answer:**

1. Click the **mic button**. It turns red and the timer starts.
2. Speak your answer naturally, as if you're in the room with an interviewer.
3. Click the mic again to stop. Fix any speech-to-text errors in the transcript.
4. Review the filler-word count — aim for **fewer than 5** in a 2-minute answer.
5. No mic available? Just type your answer in the transcript box — everything after this works identically.

## The AI coaching loop (step by step)

This is the heart of the tool. When you click **Generate AI coaching prompt** in the practice modal, the tool builds a structured prompt, copies it to your clipboard, and shows an on-screen **"What to do next"** guide. The loop has two distinct copy-paste hops — don't mix them up:

```
1. Copy the generated prompt
2. Paste it into your AI assistant (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) — a separate tab
3. Copy the AI's ENTIRE reply, including the [READINESS SCORES] block at the bottom
4. Paste that reply into the Readiness tab -> "Log a rep" -> Parse & log this rep
```

Step by step:

1. In the practice modal, click **Generate AI coaching prompt**. The prompt appears and is copied to your clipboard automatically (a "Copied" confirmation shows). There's a **Copy prompt again** button if you need it.
2. Open your **AI assistant** in a new browser tab — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or whichever you use — and **paste the prompt** as a new message.
3. When it replies, **copy the AI's entire response**, including the `[READINESS SCORES] … [END READINESS SCORES]` block at the very bottom.
4. Come back to the playbook, open the **Readiness** tab, paste the reply into **"Log a rep,"** and click **Parse & log this rep**. Your dashboard updates automatically.

> 💡 The prompt is **AI-agnostic** — it works with any capable assistant. The scored feedback comes from the AI; the tool only parses and tracks it. This is what keeps the playbook free and requires no API key.

## Readiness dashboard

The **Readiness** tab turns practice into measurable progress, built from the AI coaching scores you paste in across every rep you log. Until you log your first rep, an **empty-state message** explains exactly how to fill it.

**The eight competency dimensions:**

- Problem structuring & root-cause rigor
- Operational & technical knowledge
- Quantitative & metrics fluency
- Process-improvement application (Lean/Six Sigma)
- Trade-off & business judgment
- Leadership & people management
- Stakeholder communication & executive presence
- Recommendation & implementation focus

Each dimension is scored 0–100 per rep. The dashboard shows your running average per dimension and your overall average across all scored reps.

**The score block** at the bottom of the AI's reply looks like this:

```
[READINESS SCORES]
Problem structuring & root-cause rigor: 74
Operational & technical knowledge: 81
Quantitative & metrics fluency: 66
Process-improvement application (Lean/Six Sigma): 70
Trade-off & business judgment: 78
Leadership & people management: NA
Stakeholder communication & executive presence: 72
Recommendation & implementation focus: 80
[END READINESS SCORES]
```

The parser is tolerant — it handles reordered lines, colon/dash/equals separators, labels with or without parentheses, and skips any line marked `NA`. **Copy the full reply**; you don't need to isolate the block.

> ⚠️ **If the dashboard says no scores were found**, your AI likely dropped or reformatted the block. Paste the *entire* reply, or ask your AI: *"Please add the [READINESS SCORES] block exactly as specified in the prompt."*

**Readiness levels:**

| Level | Overall score | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Emerging** | < 45 | Early reps — focus on structure and fundamentals. |
| **Developing** | 45 – 67 | Real progress — close the weak competencies. |
| **Interview-Ready** | 68 – 84 | Solid and consistent — keep sharpening. |
| **Manager-Caliber** | ≥ 85 | Performing at the bar for the role. |

**Dimensions scored `NA`:** If the AI marks a dimension `NA` (the question didn't exercise it), that rep doesn't count toward that dimension's average. Only reps where a dimension was actually scored 0–100 contribute — keeping the dashboard honest.

## Saving, exporting & importing progress

Logged reps and saved notes are written to your browser's **localStorage**, which persists across sessions on the same browser and device.

The Readiness tab has three data-management buttons:

- **Export progress (JSON)** — downloads a file (`ops-interview-progress.json`) with all your logged reps and saved notes. Use it to back up or move to another device.
- **Import** — loads a previously exported JSON file and merges the data into your current session.
- **Reset all** — clears all logged reps and saved notes after a confirmation prompt. **Permanent and cannot be undone.**

> ⚠️ **Export before switching browsers.** Progress lives in the browser's localStorage. If you clear browser data, switch browsers, or use a private/incognito window, your data won't be there.

## Suggested workflow

**Phase 1 — Ground yourself (Days 1–3).** Skim all 15 Battlecard groups. Read through Frameworks and Quant Drills. Check the Red Flags tab so you know the mistakes before you make them.

**Phase 2 — Work the questions by category (Days 4–10).** Use the Category filter in Questions. Read Conversational and Deep Dive first, then Practice, record, generate the coaching prompt, run it in your AI, and log the rep. Do Foundational and Core before Advanced and Expert.

**Phase 3 — Run the cases (Days 8–14).** Open a Scenario, study the exhibit, and talk through your answer out loud for 3–5 minutes *before* reading the strong-answer path. Forming your own hypothesis first is where the learning happens.

**Phase 4 — Sharpen weak dimensions (Ongoing).** After 10+ logged reps, your dashboard shows a clear pattern. Target your two weakest competency dimensions with specific questions and scenarios.

**Phase 5 — Pre-interview day.** Review the Battlecard groups, run the Red Flags checklist, and warm up with 2–3 Leadership prompts in practice mode. Your current Readiness level is your honest baseline.

> 💡 **One rep a day beats a weekend marathon.** The competency averages are most useful when built from varied questions across categories. Spread practice across all eight tabs over days, not hours.

## Deploying to GitHub Pages

Hosting the tool on GitHub Pages gives you a clean `https://` URL — which enables the microphone and lets you share it.

1. Add the HTML file to your repository, named **`index.html`**.
2. Go to **Settings → Pages**.
3. Set **Source** appropriately (Deploy from a branch → `main` → `/ (root)`, or GitHub Actions if you use a custom workflow), and Save.
4. The tool goes live at `https://<username>.github.io/<repo-name>/` within about a minute.

> If a deploy fails with *"Deployment failed, try again later,"* that's usually a transient GitHub infrastructure error — re-run the failed job; no code change needed.

## FAQ

<details>
<summary><strong>Does this tool require an internet connection?</strong></summary>

No. The HTML file is fully self-contained and works offline after the first load. The only features needing connectivity are loading the web fonts on first open (they fall back gracefully if offline) and the AI coaching step, which needs a browser tab open to your AI assistant.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>My microphone isn't working. What should I do?</strong></summary>

The speech recognition uses the browser's Web Speech API, which requires Chrome or Edge (not Firefox or Safari) and a page served over `https://` or `localhost`. The live GitHub Pages link works; a local `file://` path often blocks the mic. Either way, just type your answer — the coaching workflow is identical.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Which AI assistant should I use for coaching?</strong></summary>

Any capable assistant works — ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini. The generated prompt is AI-agnostic. Paste it as a new message, let the AI finish, then copy the entire reply (including the `[READINESS SCORES]` block) back into the Readiness tab.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>I logged a rep but the dashboard didn't change. Why?</strong></summary>

The dashboard needs the `[READINESS SCORES]` block in the pasted text. If it's missing, the tool shows a clear message and nothing is logged. Paste the AI's *entire* reply, or ask the AI to add the block exactly as specified in the prompt. If every line came back `NA`, there's nothing to log for that rep.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Will my progress be saved if I close the browser?</strong></summary>

Yes — logged reps and saved notes are stored in your browser's localStorage and persist across sessions on the same browser and device. Export your progress as a JSON backup before clearing any browser data.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Can I use this on my phone or tablet?</strong></summary>

Yes — the tool is responsive. The tab bar scrolls horizontally on narrow screens, the transcript box is touch-editable, and the dashboard works normally. Speech recognition on mobile Chrome works over the `https://` live link.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Can I print the Battlecards or Red Flags?</strong></summary>

Yes. Use your browser's Print function (Ctrl/Cmd + P) while on the Battlecard or Red Flags tab, and print to PDF for a take-anywhere reference.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>I scored low on one dimension. What should I practice?</strong></summary>

Each competency maps to specific content:
- **Quantitative fluency** -> Quant Drills + Lean/CI and Manufacturing question categories
- **Problem structuring** -> all Scenario cases + Process Improvement questions
- **Technical knowledge** -> Operations Strategy, Inventory, Procurement, and Logistics categories
- **Leadership & people management** -> Leadership tab
- **Judgment & trade-offs** -> Expert-difficulty scenarios and questions

Use the Category and Difficulty filters to target exactly where you need reps.
</details>
